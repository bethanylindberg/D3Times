A review of relationships between health risks and certain descriptive demographics

The chart above visualizes the relationship between several independent or manipulated (x-axis) variables and dependent or responding (y-axis) variables, measured by state in the US. As always, correlation does not imply causation as there could be any number of factors contributing to both figures.

Median age in the states does not have a correlation to average smokers, citizens without access to healthcare or percentage of the population that are obese.

Median income in a state has a mild negative linear relationship with percentage of the population without access to healthcare and percentage of the population that are obese while there is no relationship between median income and lack of access to healthcare.

Percentage of the population of a state residing in poverty has a weak relationship with all three responding variables, with all three showing a slightly positive association.
